Sprint 9

User group:

Supervising engineers

What we tested

  • How might the prototype fit with their current ways of working?
  • What impact do they anticipate from using the new system? (positive and negative)
  • What challenges do they see with integrating the new process into the way they do things?
  • What might improve the prototype?
  • What opportunities do they see for a digital service (future thinking)?
  • Does the prototype allow for them to include all the information they would want to? (anything missing)
  • What information might they want to attach? (site visit reports)
  • What order does the information make most sense?
  • What are their thoughts on the quality of the service output for owners vs the report they would have originally created?
  • How well do they feel the output represents their work?
  • Are their concerns about the output that the owner will receive?

What we learned

  • This version of the prototype does not fit with the SE's current ways of working
  • This view this prototype as a duplication of work
  • The languauge should be more specific
  • The S12 report requires details from the S10
  • The system should allow for colloborative ways of working
  • There are concerns about internet and access
  • The SE's are concerened about compliance questions

Version 2

Sprint 2 - 3

User group:

Supervising engineers

What we tested

  • can a user complete the end-to-end-journey
  • do they understand the contnent and the questions

What we learned

  • Choose a reservoir – users did not want to scroll through a list of potentially 2000 reservoirs
  • Issues to be watched and maintenance actions – users found that having a free text input would not work as there could be more than one issue or action to list
  • Issues to be watched and maintenance actions – users want to itemise all the issues or actions, to show whether the operator is being complinat with the process
  • New or abandoned reservoir – users did not understand the question. They wondered if the statement referred to a new or exisiting reservoir?
  • Operator keeping records and monitoring the reservior – users did not feel a yes or no was adequate. They would need to be able to give advice to the owners and operators as well as issue mandatory requirements.
Version 1

Sprint 1

User group:

Supervising engineers

Assumptions

  1. Our users will find it easy to confirm or update pre-populated information.
  2. Giving users pre-populated information will save them time and help improve the accuracy of data held by the Environment Agency.

The scenarios

  • confirm the Reservoir, Operator and last inspector details
  • change the Reservoir and Operator details
  • add Operator details and change Reservoir details

What we tested

  • user can confirm the information on the screen
  • user can change the current information on the screen
  • user can input new information and save it

What we learned

  • Choose a reservoir – users did not want to scroll through a list of potentially 2000 reservoirs
  • Operator details – some users were happy with the term 'operator' and some were not
