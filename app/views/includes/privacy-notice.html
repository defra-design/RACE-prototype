Privacy notice: how we use your data

The Environment Agency is the enforcement authority for reservoir law in England. We are the controller of the data collected and processed through our Send a supervising engineer's annual statement service (digital service) and Word template.

A data controller decides how and why personal data is processed.

Our personal information charter explains your rights and how we deal with your personal data in general.

What data we collect

We collect personal data from supervising engineers who create accounts for and use the digital service and Word template.

This data includes:

  • your name
  • your address, phone number, email address (if you're a sole trader)
  • your company name, address, phone number and email address (if you're employed)

The legal basis for processing your personal data is the Environment Agency's legal obligation as enforcement authority.

Your responsibility with other people's personal data

If you've included personal data about other people on your application, you must tell them.

You must provide them with a copy of this privacy notice so that they know how their personal data will be used.

Why we need your personal data

As the enforcement authority we need your personal data to make sure that only registered supervising engineers can access the service and send annual statements, and that you can only do this for the reservoirs you're legally appointed to.

We also need to communicate with you about your statements and issues of reservoir safety.

What we do with your personal data

We use your personal data to:

  • check that you're allowed to use this service
  • pre-populate your annual statement template in the future
  • process your annual statement
  • check that the details we hold about you are up to date and update if necessary
  • your address, phone number, email address (if you're a sole trader)
  • send email reminders, notices and warnings
  • contact you if we have questions about your annual statement or the reservoir it relates to
  • communicate information about annual statements, using GOV.Notify
  • enforce the law

We might share your data we collect with Defra. It might also be shared with our technology suppliers, for example our hosting provider.

We will share your personal data if we are required to do so by law, for example by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

We will not:

  • sell or rent your personal data with third parties
  • share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes

Giving feedback about the service

You might be asked to provide feedback or take part in research about the digital service and Word template. We use this feedback to improve our systems.

The legal basis for processing this data is because you give your consent.

You can withdraw your consent at any time.

How long we keep your personal data

We keep any data you enter in the digital service for 2 years.

The personal data you send in the annual statement via the digital service and Word template which is entered into the Reservoir Act Compliance and Enforcement system is kept indefinitely as it forms an historical record.

Where your data is processed and stored

We design, build and run our systems to make sure that your data is as safe as possible at any stage, both while it's processed and when it's stored.

Your personal data will be processed and stored in the UK.

Questions and complaints

Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for independent advice and monitoring of the Environment Agency's use of personal data.
